# A. Frank Martin (Andrew Frank Martin)
## "Mr. Kappa Kappa Psi" - The Ritual Master

---

## Quick Facts
- **Full Name:** A. "Andrew" Frank Martin
- **Nickname:** "Mr. Kappa Kappa Psi"
- **Born:** December 7, 1894
- **Birthplace:** Sallisaw, Oklahoma
- **Class Year:** 1920
- **Major:** Education
- **Instrument:** Saxophone
- **Death Date:** June 9, 1982
- **Age at Death:** 87
- **Burial Location:** Stillwater, Oklahoma

---

## Fun Facts

### 1. **The Title Says It All**
A. Frank Martin earned the nickname **"Mr. Kappa Kappa Psi"** — a testament to his deep connection to the fraternity's identity and his monumental contributions.

### 2. **The Ritual Author**
A. Frank Martin is credited as the **author of the Ritual of Kappa Kappa Psi**. Very few revisions have been made to his original vision, meaning his words and concepts have endured for over 100 years.

### 3. **First Grand President**
After being the local Vice President at Oklahoma A&M, Martin was elected as the **first Grand President of Kappa Kappa Psi** (1919-1922), serving for 3 years during the critical founding period.

### 4. **Educated Educator**
Martin was the only founding father with "Education" as his major, making him uniquely qualified to shape the fraternity's educational mission and philosophy.

### 5. **Saxophonist in a Brass Band World**
While most founding fathers played brass instruments, Martin played the **saxophone** — bringing a different tonal color and perspective to the band.

### 6. **Multi-Talented Renaissance Man**
Martin wasn't just a musician. His college activities included:
- Social Editor
- Student Senate
- Bandmaster (1919-1920)
- Managing Editor of the Redskin yearbook
- Vice President of Education Society

### 7. **Naval Service**
During World War I, Martin joined the Navy and played in a band in New Orleans—the same band where William H. Coppedge also served! They bonded through shared military musical experience.

### 8. **Second Career Success**
After leaving Oklahoma A&M following his Grand Presidency, Martin had a stellar career:
- Music Director at Columbia University
- Member of Phi Mu Alpha
- Reorganized Long Island University's Music Department
- Helped colonize the Beta Zeta chapter of Kappa Kappa Psi
- Served as District IX (Northeast) Counselor/Governor

### 9. **Longevity and Recognition**
Martin lived to be 87 years old and was even more active in his later years:
- Returned as the **Third Executive Secretary** (1939-1964)
- Served the fraternity for 45+ years in various capacities

### 10. **The Right Hand**
While Scroggs had the vision, Martin had the structures. His Ritual, Constitution work, and organizational frameworks became the bones of the fraternity that still stand today.

## Timeline

### December 7, 1894
**Birth** - A. Frank Martin is born in Sallisaw, Oklahoma.

### 1918-1920
**College Years Begin** - Martin attends Oklahoma A&M College and becomes active in band and student life.

### 1919-1920
**Chosen as Vice President** - During the secret conference arranged by William Scroggs, A. Frank Martin is selected as one of the ten charter members and elected as Vice President of the local organization.

### 1919-1920
**Leads Ritual Creation** - As a member of the Ritual Committee (along with Clayton Soule and Col. F.D. Wickham), Martin authors the Ritual of Kappa Kappa Psi, establishing ceremonial practices that endure over 100 years.

### 1920
**Graduates** - A. Frank Martin completes his education and graduates from Oklahoma A&M (Class of 1920).

### 1919-1922
**First Grand President** - Martin is elected as the first Grand President of Kappa Kappa Psi and serves during the critical founding and early expansion period.

### 1920-1921
**Military Service** - Martin joins the Navy during World War I and serves in a military band in New Orleans, where he reunites with fellow charter member William Coppedge.

### 1922
**Transition** - Martin concludes his term as Grand President after establishing the foundational structures of the fraternity.

### 1920s-1930s
**Columbia University Years** - Martin becomes Music Director at Columbia University and expands his influence in music education.

### 1930s-1940s
**Continued Service** - Martin maintains involvement with the fraternity while building his career in music education.

### 1939
**Returns as Executive Secretary** - Martin returns to formal fraternity service as the Third Executive Secretary, a role he will hold for 25 years.

### 1939-1964
**Executive Secretary Tenure** - During these 25 years, Martin helps reorganize Long Island University's Music Department, becomes a member of Phi Mu Alpha, and helps establish the Beta Zeta chapter of KKPsi at Long Island University.

### 1945-1950s
**District Service** - Martin serves as District IX (Northeast) Counselor/Governor, expanding the fraternity's reach and influence on the East Coast.

### June 9, 1982
**Death** - A. Frank Martin, the "Mr. Kappa Kappa Psi" who gave the fraternity its soul through his Ritual, passes away at age 87. He is buried in Stillwater, Oklahoma — returning home to where it all began.

## Legacy

A. Frank Martin is the architect of Kappa Kappa Psi's spiritual and ceremonial foundation. His Ritual established the fraternity not just as an organization, but as a community with shared values and sacred practices.

Martin's **45+ years of service** to the fraternity—from founding member to first Grand President to Executive Secretary—make him one of the most instrumental figures in KKPsi history. His work as "Mr. Kappa Kappa Psi" continues to shape every initiate who goes through the Ritual he created over a century ago.

The fraternity's identity, its ceremonies, its organizational structure—all bear the fingerprints of A. Frank Martin's thoughtful design and leadership.
