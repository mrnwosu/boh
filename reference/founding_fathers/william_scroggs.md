# William Alexander Scroggs
## The Founder of Kappa Kappa Psi

---

## Quick Facts
- **Full Name:** William Alexander Scroggs
- **Nickname:** "The Founder"
- **Birth Year:** Unknown
- **Class Year:** 1922
- **Hometown:** Stillwater, Oklahoma
- **Major:** Commerce and Marketing
- **Instrument:** Cornet
- **Death Date:** March 14, 1984
- **Burial Location:** San Diego, California

---

## Fun Facts

### 1. **Perfect Timing**
William Scroggs' birthday fell on **November 27th** — the exact same date that Kappa Kappa Psi received its official charter! This makes his birthday eternally connected to the fraternity's founding date.

### 2. **The Spark of an Idea**
In the fall of 1919, while other band members were content with the status quo, William Scroggs alone conceived the revolutionary idea of creating a national fraternity exclusively for college bandsmen. No one else in the band had this vision.

### 3. **Smart Approach to Authority**
Rather than acting on his own idea, Scroggs was wise enough to present it to authority figures first:
- He confided in A. Frank Martin (President of the Band)
- He then approached Professor Bohumil Makovsky (Director of Bands)
- This strategic approach ensured institutional support from the very beginning

### 4. **Visionary Organizer**
Scroggs didn't just dream—he acted. He arranged a secret conference with Professor Makovsky and A. Frank Martin to select the other nine charter members. This shows his leadership quality of bringing the right people together.

### 5. **First President**
Scroggs was unanimously elected as the **first President** of Kappa Kappa Psi at the local level—a testament to how his peers respected his leadership.

### 6. **Dual Roles**
While serving as the first local President, Scroggs also held the national position of **National Editor and Assistant Secretary**, showing he was invested in both chapter and national success.

### 7. **Long Life Span**
Scroggs lived to be over 90 years old (born unknown, died March 14, 1984), giving him decades to witness the fraternity's growth and expansion.

### 8. **Post-Fraternity Success**
After his time at Oklahoma A&M, Scroggs had a successful career and eventually settled in California. He maintained connection to the fraternity while building a life in the West.

### 9. **Legacy Awards**
In honor of William Alexander Scroggs, Kappa Kappa Psi created the **William A. Scroggs Founder's Trophy**, ensuring his memory is celebrated in perpetuity.

### 10. **The Humble Founder**
Despite being THE founder, Scroggs was humble enough to call in A. Frank Martin as Vice President and Bohumil Makovsky as the guiding force. True leadership recognizes when to elevate others.

## Timeline

### Fall 1919
**Conceives Fraternity Idea** - William Scroggs, a member of the Oklahoma A&M College Band, conceives the idea that something should be done to bring together bandsmen in colleges and universities.

### Fall 1919 (Early)
**Presents Idea to A. Frank Martin** - Scroggs confides in Mr. A. Frank Martin, President of the Band, with his plans for organizing a National Band Fraternity. Martin is enthusiastic.

### Fall 1919 (Mid)
**Approaches Professor Makovsky** - Knowing that Professor Bohumil Makovsky strongly supported the development of better music, Scroggs presents his proposition. Makovsky immediately sees the possibilities and offers undivided support.

### Fall 1919 (Late)
**Secret Conference Held** - Scroggs arranges a secret conference with Professor Makovsky and A. Frank Martin where ten of the best men from the Oklahoma A&M College Band are selected as charter members.

### Fall 1919 (Final)
**Elected First President** - William Scroggs is unanimously elected as the first President of the local Kappa Kappa Psi organization.

### November 27, 1919
**Charter Granted** - Kappa Kappa Psi receives its official charter from the Oklahoma Corporation Commission. The local group becomes "The Alpha Chapter." Scroggs' birthday.

### 1919-1920
**Serves in Multiple Roles** - Scroggs serves as:
- Local Chapter President
- National Editor and Assistant Secretary (first national officers)

### 1920-1921
**Expansion Phase** - Scroggs helps with the expansion of the fraternity as petitions begin arriving from other institutions.

### 1922
**Continued Service** - Scroggs remains active in fraternity affairs and completes his undergraduate education (Class of 1922).

### 1920s-1980s
**Life After College** - Scroggs builds a successful career, eventually settling in California while maintaining fraternity involvement.

### March 14, 1984
**Death** - William Alexander Scroggs, the founder of Kappa Kappa Psi, passes away after living a full life and witnessing nearly 65 years of the fraternity's growth and development.

## Legacy

William Alexander Scroggs is remembered as the visionary founder who had the courage to dream of a national fraternity and the wisdom to seek support from those who could help him achieve it. His idea, born from a desire to unite college bandsmen, has touched the lives of thousands of musicians across multiple generations.

The **William A. Scroggs Founder's Trophy** ensures that his legacy lives on within the fraternity, and November 27th will forever connect his birthday to the birth of Kappa Kappa Psi.
