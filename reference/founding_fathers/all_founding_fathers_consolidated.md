# Kappa Kappa Psi Founding Fathers - Consolidated
## Complete Facts & Master Timeline

## Overview

On **November 27, 1919**, ten young musicians at Oklahoma A&M College came together with a vision: to create a national fraternity that would elevate the status of college bands and unite bandsmen across America. What they built—with complementary skills, shared values, and remarkable collaborative spirit—has endured for over 100 years.

This document consolidates the stories, facts, and achievements of all ten founding fathers, showing how their individual talents combined to create one of the most significant student organizations in music education.

## The Ten Founding Fathers - Key Facts & Stories

### 1. **William Alexander Scroggs** — "The Founder"
**Hometown:** Stillwater, Oklahoma | **Major:** Commerce & Marketing | **Instrument:** Cornet | **Lived:** 1884(?)-1984

**Key Achievement:** Conceived the entire idea of founding Kappa Kappa Psi

**Notable Fun Facts:**
- His birthday fell on **November 27** — the exact same date KKPsi received its charter!
- Alone conceived the revolutionary idea while his peers accepted the status quo
- Strategically approached authority figures first (A. Frank Martin, then Professor Makovsky) before proceeding
- Arranged the secret conference that selected all ten charter members
- First Local President, unanimously elected by his peers
- Also served as National Editor and Assistant Secretary
- Lived over 90 years (1884-1984), witnessing 65 years of fraternity growth
- Had a successful post-college career in California
- Honored with the **William A. Scroggs Founder's Trophy**

### 2. **A. Frank Martin** — "Mr. Kappa Kappa Psi"
**Hometown:** Sallisaw, Oklahoma | **Major:** Education | **Instrument:** Saxophone | **Lived:** 1894-1982 (age 87)

**Key Achievement:** Authored the Ritual of Kappa Kappa Psi

**Notable Fun Facts:**
- Earned the legendary title **"Mr. Kappa Kappa Psi"** due to his monumental contributions
- **Author of the Ritual** — His original vision survives with minimal changes over 100+ years
- First Grand President (1919-1922) during the critical founding period
- Only founding father with Education as major — uniquely qualified for fraternity's educational mission
- Played saxophone while most others played brass — bringing different perspective

- Served in U.S. Navy during WWI; reunited with William Coppedge in military band in New Orleans
- Exceptional post-college career: Music Director at Columbia University, helped establish Beta Zeta chapter at Long Island University, District IX Counselor
- **45+ years of service** including returning as Third Executive Secretary (1939-1964)
- Buried in Stillwater — returning home to where it all began

## Master Timeline - All Founding Fathers

### **1894**
- **December 7** — A. Frank Martin is born in Sallisaw, Oklahoma

### **1896**
- Clayton E. Soule is born in Nowata, Oklahoma

### **1897**
- **Spring** — Raymond D. Shannon is born in Perkins, Oklahoma
- **Spring** — Carl A. Stevens is born in Quapaw, Oklahoma

### **1898**
- William H. Coppedge is born in Grove, Oklahoma
- George Asher Hendrickson is born in Boyton, Oklahoma
- Dick Hurst is born in Oklahoma City, Oklahoma
- Iron Hawthorne Nelson is born in Stillwater, Oklahoma

### **1919 - Fall (Early)**
**Scroggs Conceives the Vision**
- William Alexander Scroggs, a member of Oklahoma A&M College Band, conceives the revolutionary idea of creating a national fraternity for college bandsmen
- No other band member shares this vision; Scroggs alone sees the possibility

### **1919 - Fall (Mid-September)**
**Strategic Leadership Approach**
- Scroggs confides in A. Frank Martin (President of the Oklahoma A&M College Band)
- Martin is immediately enthusiastic about the concept
- Together, they recognize they need institutional support

### **1919 - Fall (Late September)**
**Institutional Blessing Secured**
- Scroggs and Martin approach Professor Bohumil Makovsky (Director of Bands)
- Makovsky immediately endorses the concept and offers undivided support
- The three leaders recognize this venture could transform college band music nationally

### **1919 - Fall (September 28-29)**
**The Secret Conference**
- William Scroggs arranges a secret conference with Professor Makovsky and A. Frank Martin
- Ten of the best young musicians from Oklahoma A&M College Band are selected as charter members:
  - William Alexander Scroggs
  - A. Frank Martin
  - Raymond D. Shannon
  - Carl A. Stevens
  - Clyde Haston
  - Clayton E. Soule
  - William H. Coppedge
  - George Asher Hendrickson
  - Dick Hurst
  - Iron Hawthorne Nelson
- Each is informed individually of their selection

### **1919 - October**
**First Meetings and Organization**
- The ten charter members meet formally
- Officers are elected:
  - **William Alexander Scroggs** — Local President
  - **A. Frank Martin** — Vice President
  - **Raymond D. Shannon** — First Vice President (National)
  - **Carl A. Stevens** — Treasurer
  - **Clyde Haston** — Second Vice President (National)
  - **Clayton E. Soule** — Secretary
  - **William H. Coppedge** — Sergeant of Arms
  - **George Asher Hendrickson** — National Treasurer
  - **Dick Hurst** — National Secretary
  - **Iron Hawthorne Nelson** — Constitution and Bylaws Committee
- William Scroggs also serves as National Editor and Assistant Secretary

### **1919 - October (Mid)**
**Ritual Committee Begins Work**
- A. Frank Martin leads the Ritual Committee (with Clayton Soule and Colonel F.D. Wickham)
- They begin creating the ceremonial structure and ritual language for initiation
- Martin's educational background proves invaluable in shaping spiritual and educational content

### **1919 - October (Late)**
**Badge Design Committee Convenes**
- Clyde Haston, William H. Coppedge, and Dick Hurst form the Badge Design Committee
- They work to create a visual symbol that embodies the fraternity's values
- Their design will feature the Crown, Pearl, and Greek letters ΚΚΨ

### **1919 - November**
**Constitution & Bylaws Development**
- Iron Hawthorne Nelson leads constitutional work with others
- Raymond D. Shannon contributes to constitutional framework for national expansion
- They create governance structures that will allow chapters to coordinate nationally
- Carl A. Stevens and George Asher Hendrickson develop financial procedures

### **November 27, 1919 (Wednesday)**
**CHARTER GRANTED - KAPPA KAPPA PSI IS OFFICIALLY FOUNDED**
- Oklahoma Corporation Commission officially grants charter to Kappa Kappa Psi
- The local group becomes officially recognized as "The Alpha Chapter"
- **This is William Alexander Scroggs' birthday** — cementing his connection to the founding
- The Crown Pearl Badge design is finalized
- The Ritual of A. Frank Martin is ceremonially approved
- The Constitution written by Iron Hawthorne Nelson and colleagues is ratified
- All ten founding fathers are officially recognized as charter members

### **1919-1920 Academic Year**

## Historical Context - Why 1919 Mattered

These ten men founded Kappa Kappa Psi during one of the most turbulent years in American history:

- **World War I** had just ended (1918)
- **Women gained the right to vote** (19th Amendment ratified)
- **Spanish Flu pandemic** was killing millions
- **"Red Scare"** and labor unrest were spreading fear
- **Economic uncertainty** — the post-war economy was unstable

**Yet amidst this chaos, these ten college musicians created an organization dedicated to brotherhood, musicianship, and service.**

This speaks volumes about their character, vision, and commitment to something larger than themselves.
