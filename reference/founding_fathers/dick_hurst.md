# Dick Hurst
## The Secretary and Badge Designer

---

## Quick Facts
- **Full Name:** Dick Hurst
- **Class Year:** 1922
- **Hometown:** Oklahoma City, Oklahoma
- **Major:** Commerce and Marketing
- **Instrument:** Cornet
- **First Role:** Band President (1922)
- **National Role:** National Secretary
- **Later Role:** Grand Secretary (1922-1923)
- **Death Date:** Unknown

---

## Fun Facts

### 1. **Band Leadership**
Before becoming involved in the fraternity, Dick Hurst was already a campus leader, serving as **President of the College Band** during 1922 — his class year.

### 2. **Multiple Leadership Positions**
Hurst held numerous leadership roles both in college and in the fraternity:
- Freshman Class Secretary-Treasurer
- College Band President (1922)
- Drum Major (1920-1922)
- National Secretary (among first National Officers)
- Grand Secretary (1922-1923)

This shows his widespread respect and trustworthiness.

### 3. **Badge Design Committee**
Hurst served on the **Fraternity Pin Design and Coat of Arms Committee** alongside Clyde Haston and William H. Coppedge, helping create the fraternity's iconic visual symbol.

### 4. **Cornet Player**
Like William Scroggs, Hurst played the **cornet** — a brass instrument that provides bright, clear melody in the band.

### 5. **Drum Major Experience**
Hurst's experience as Drum Major (1920-1922) gave him conducting and leadership experience, preparing him for his roles in the fraternity's governance.

### 6. **Oklahoma City Native**
Hurst was one of the few founding fathers who came from Oklahoma's largest city, bringing urban perspective to the predominantly rural group.

### 7. **Multi-Campus Involvement**
Beyond his work with the College Band, Hurst was involved in:
- Student Senate
- Wildcat (student newspaper)
- Enterprise Association
- Redskin Board and Staff
- Lambda Chi Alpha social fraternity
- Alpha Kappa Psi professional fraternity

This shows he was a truly engaged student leader.

### 8. **Respected Peers**
The fact that Hurst was elected Freshman Class Secretary-Treasurer shows that even as a young student, he earned the trust of his classmates.

### 9. **National Expansion Role**
As National Secretary, Hurst was involved in the critical early decisions about how the fraternity would expand and communicate with new chapters.

### 10. **Quick Rise**
From the time he was selected as a charter member in fall 1919 to his election as Grand Secretary in 1922-1923, Hurst rose quickly in the fraternity's ranks, showing his capability and dedication.

## Timeline

### 1919-1920
**Freshman Year Leadership** - Dick Hurst is elected as **Secretary-Treasurer of the Freshman Class**, establishing himself as a campus leader while his college years are just beginning.

### 1920-1922
**Drum Major Service** - Hurst serves as **Drum Major** of the College Band (1920-1922), gaining leadership and conducting experience that will serve him in the fraternity.

### Fall 1919
**Selected as Charter Member** - Hurst is selected as one of the ten charter members of Kappa Kappa Psi.

### 1919
**First National Officers Elected** - Hurst is elected as **National Secretary** among the first National Officers.

### 1919-1920
**Badge Design Committee** - Hurst serves on the Fraternity Pin Design and Coat of Arms Committee with Clyde Haston and William H. Coppedge, contributing to the design of the fraternity's iconic badge.

### 1922
**Elected Band President** - In his senior year, Hurst is elected **President of the College Band**.

### 1922
**Graduation** - Dick Hurst completes his studies and graduates in the Class of 1922.

### 1922-1923
**Grand Secretary** - Hurst is elected as **Grand Secretary** at or shortly after the First National Convention, continuing his secretarial responsibilities at the national level.

### 1920s and Beyond
**Continued Involvement** - Hurst remains active in the fraternity as it expands and develops throughout the 1920s and beyond.

---

## Legacy

Dick Hurst represents the multi-talented, campus-engaged founding father who brought leadership skills developed across many organizations to the creation of Kappa Kappa Psi.

His work as National Secretary and later Grand Secretary made him the keeper of the fraternity's growing organizational records during the critical expansion period. His involvement in the badge design committee connected him to one of KKPsi's most enduring symbols.

Hurst's quick rise through fraternity leadership — from charter member to National Secretary to Grand Secretary in just a few years — shows how valuable his skills were to the young organization. He was one of the most active founding fathers in shaping the fraternity's early institutional structure.
