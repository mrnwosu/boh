# Bohumil Makovsky: A Life Timeline

## Early Life in Bohemia (1878-1895)

### September 23, 1878
**Born in Frantisky, Bohemia** (now part of Czech Republic) to Václav and Anna Hladik Makovsky. His father died before he was born.

### 1890
**Mother Passes Away** at age 12. Bohumil is left largely on his own but continues living on the family estate inherited by his older brother.

### 1895
**Immigrates to the United States** at age 17 with help from his older sister, Anna Brdicka, who had settled in Clarkson, Nebraska the year before. Makes the four-day voyage in steerage with minimal food and money.

---

## Settlement & Early Career in America (1895-1910)

### 1895
**First Job in America** - Settles in Clarkson, Nebraska with his sister Anna and finds employment rolling cigars in a shop owned by a local cigar maker.

### Mid-1890s (c. 1895-1896)
**First Professional Music Job: Edward Reno's Traveling Wagon Show** - When magician Edward Reno's traveling wagon show comes through Clarkson, Reno hires Boh as a clarinet player at $6 per week, marking the beginning of his career as a professional musician in the United States.

### 1895-1900
**Early Years in Nebraska and Oklahoma** - Takes on various jobs including farm work and musical performances. Supplements income through manual labor while establishing himself as a musician.

### c. 1900
**First Band Formed** - Forms his own band at the turn of the century after several years as a sideman in various organizations. Contracts to bring the group to Oklahoma in 1902, but the venture fails when a carnival in Davis, Oklahoma refuses to pay. Feeling obligated to the band members, Boh pays them out of his own pocket before disbanding, leaving him broke.

### Early 1900s
**Oklahoma City Years Begin** - After the failed band venture, makes his way to Oklahoma City. Fills his days teaching private music lessons in the morning, playing in a theater in the afternoons, and directing his own concert band and orchestra at the newly opened Delmar Gardens at night.

### 1907-1908
**Band Director for Multiple Communities** - Organizes and directs bands in Mustang, Yukon, Prague, and Shawnee, Oklahoma before turning them over to local directors.

### July 18, 1908
**First Composition Premiered: "King of Oklahoma March"** - Boh's first march is premiered with the Commercial Club Band, a group he founded earlier that year on an eight-month contract with local businessmen.

### August 2, 1911
**Marriage** to Georgia Shestack (also of Bohemian descent), whom he met while visiting his family in Nebraska. Georgia becomes an important source of encouragement throughout his life.

### 1912
**Forms the Makovsky Concert Band** - Establishes his own professional concert band. Resigns position with the Oklahoma City Metropolitan Band to pursue this venture.

### 1910 - 1922
**Oklahoma State Fair Association** (12-year tenure) - Begins conducting the Oklahoma City Metropolitan Band at the Oklahoma State Fair. Performs approximately 10 concerts in 3 months with rarely repeated programs. Achieves significant public exposure through Sunday concerts at Fair Park.

---

## OAMC Years: The Defining Era (1915-1945)

### Fall 1915
**Joins Oklahoma Agricultural and Mechanical College (OAMC)** - Appointed Director of Bands and Head of the Music Department following the sudden illness and death of Joseph Watson. Works under new college president J. W. Cantwell, who advocates a new attitude toward music education.

### 1918
**International Recognition** - Listed in the International Who's Who, marking his rising prominence as an educator and musician.

### Fall 1919 - January 2, 1922
**Mentors the Founding of Kappa Kappa Psi**
- **Fall 1919**: William A. Scroggs approaches Boh with the idea of founding a band fraternity. Makovsky immediately sees the great possibilities and offers his undivided support.
- Assists in selecting 10 charter members of the fraternity
- Helps shape the fraternity's founding documents and constitution
- Works with A. Frank Martin and William Scroggs on the fraternity's framework

### November 27, 1919
**Kappa Kappa Psi Chartered** - The organization receives its official charter from the Oklahoma Corporation Commission, with Bohumil Makovsky as the guiding force.

### May 1921
**Becomes U.S. Citizen** - Naturalized in Stillwater, Oklahoma, after living in America for 26 years.

### January 2, 1922
**First National Convention of Kappa Kappa Psi** held at Oklahoma A&M College in Stillwater
- Bohumil Makovsky becomes the **first National Honorary Member of Kappa Kappa Psi**
- Shortly after, John Philip Sousa also accepts honorary membership

### 1922
**Multiple Honors**
- Commissioned as an **Honorary Major** in the OAMC ROTC
- **OAMC Redskin Yearbook** dedicated to Bohumil Makovsky, recognizing his significant contributions to the college

### December 1926
**Elected Grand President of Kappa Kappa Psi** - Serves as the organization's leader during a critical period of development and expansion.

### 1927-1929
**Continues Leadership** - Serves as Grand President during the challenging financial climate of the Great Depression. Makovsky becomes one of the first nationally prominent band directors to serve in Kappa Kappa Psi leadership.

### 1931
**Published Recognition** - Listed in Pierre Key's Musical Who's Who, further cementing his status as a leading music educator.

### 1935
**State Recognition** - Listed in "Who Is Who in Oklahoma" for his contributions to the state's cultural and educational development.

### 1939
**Oklahoma Hall of Fame** - Elected to the Oklahoma Hall of Fame in recognition of his lifetime achievements in music education and band development.

### April 9, 1940
**Wife Georgia Passes Away** - Georgia Shestack Makovsky dies after years of being "Mrs. Boh" and an important source of encouragement for her husband's work.

### 1940
**Honorary Doctorate** - Receives an **Honorary Doctor of Music Degree** from the University of Tulsa, officially recognizing his contributions to music education.

### 1945
**Retirement from OAMC** - After 30 years of service (1915-1945), Bohumil Makovsky retires from his position as Director of Bands and Head of the Music Department. Granted the title of **Head Emeritus of the Department of Music**.

---

## Legacy & Later Recognition (1945-1979+)

### June 12, 1950
**Death** - Bohumil Makovsky dies at age 71, having left an indelible mark on American music education and fraternal traditions.

### 1979
**Bohumil Makovsky Memorial Award Established** - Kappa Kappa Psi formally establishes the **Bohumil Makovsky Memorial Award** in his honor, ensuring that his legacy continues to recognize outstanding contributions within the fraternity.

## Legacy

**Dr. Bohumil Makovsky** is officially recognized by Kappa Kappa Psi as the **"Guiding Spirit"** of the fraternity. His life exemplified the values of:

- **Honor** - Conducting himself with integrity and dignity
- **Integrity** - Demonstrating unwavering principles and decency
- **Respect** - Valuing diverse students and treating them as equals
- **Loyalty** - Devoting himself to his students, bands, and the fraternity

His approach to music education—emphasizing immaculate rehearsal technique, tone and balance, symphonic sound, and strict discipline—influenced generations of musicians. Brothers of Kappa Kappa Psi are encouraged to follow Boh's example to teach and inspire others as he taught and inspired the founding fathers.
