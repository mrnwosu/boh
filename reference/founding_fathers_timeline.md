## Timeline of Events

### Fall 1919: The Conception

#### September - October 1919
**William Scroggs Conceives the Idea** - William Alexander Scroggs, a member of the Oklahoma A&M College Band, conceives the revolutionary idea of creating a national fraternity exclusively for college bandsmen. This vision comes from a desire to unite musicians and elevate the status of college bands.

#### Early Fall 1919
**Scroggs Approaches A. Frank Martin** - Scroggs confides in A. Frank Martin, President of the Band, with his plans for organizing a National Band Fraternity. Martin is enthusiastic and becomes a crucial early supporter.

#### Mid Fall 1919
**Scroggs Presents to Professor Makovsky** - Knowing that Professor Bohumil Makovsky strongly supported the development of better music, Scroggs presents his proposition. Makovsky immediately sees the great possibilities and offers his undivided support, becoming the guiding force behind the fraternity's founding.

#### Late Fall 1919
**Secret Conference** - Scroggs arranges a secret conference with Professor Makovsky and A. Frank Martin where ten of the best men from the Oklahoma A&M College Band are selected as charter members. These ten men are carefully chosen for their musical talent, character, and potential leadership.

#### November 1919 (Early)
**The Charter Members Are Selected** - The following men are selected as the ten charter members:
1. William Alexander Scroggs
2. A. Frank Martin
3. Raymond D. Shannon
4. Clayton E. Soule
5. Clyde Haston
6. William H. Coppedge
7. Dick Hurst
8. George Asher Hendrickson
9. Carl A. Stevens
10. Iron Hawthorne Nelson

#### November 1919 (Mid)
**Elected First President** - William Scroggs is unanimously elected as the first President of the local Kappa Kappa Psi organization.

#### November 27, 1919
**Official Charter Granted** - Kappa Kappa Psi receives its official charter from the Oklahoma Corporation Commission. The local group becomes "The Alpha Chapter" of Kappa Kappa Psi. **This is also William Scroggs' birthday**, making the date eternally significant in both his life and the fraternity's history.

## Individual Founder Profiles

### William Alexander Scroggs: "The Founder"

**Birth:** Unknown | **Death:** March 14, 1984 (age 90+)
**Class Year:** 1922 | **Hometown:** Stillwater, Oklahoma
**Major:** Commerce and Marketing | **Instrument:** Cornet

**Key Facts:**
- Conceived the idea of Kappa Kappa Psi in Fall 1919
- His birthday (November 27) matches the fraternity's charter date
- Unanimously elected as the first local President
- Also served as National Editor and Assistant Secretary
- For his founding role, Kappa Kappa Psi created the **William A. Scroggs Founder's Trophy**
- Lived a long life, witnessing nearly 65 years of the fraternity's growth
- Eventually settled in San Diego, California, where he was buried

**Legacy:** Recognized as THE founder of Kappa Kappa Psi for his vision and leadership in establishing the fraternity.

---

### A. Frank Martin: "Mr. Kappa Kappa Psi"

**Birth:** December 7, 1894 | **Death:** June 9, 1982 (age 87)
**Class Year:** 1920 | **Hometown:** Sallisaw, Oklahoma
**Major:** Education | **Instrument:** Saxophone

**Key Facts:**
- Served as President of the Band when Scroggs presented the idea
- Became Vice President of the initial fraternity
- First Grand President of Kappa Kappa Psi (1919-1922, serving 3 years)
- Authored the **Ritual of Kappa Kappa Psi** - his most significant contribution
- Officially titled "Mr. Kappa Kappa Psi" within fraternity history
- Listed in Pierre Key's Musical Who's Who (1931)
- Received an Honorary Doctor of Music Degree from the University of Tulsa
- Earned both bachelor's and master's degrees from Oklahoma A&M
- Served as superintendent of schools in Yale, Oklahoma and superintendent of the Boys Training School in Pauls Valley, Oklahoma
- Returned to Stillwater in 1931 to work on the university president's staff
- Served as Third Executive Secretary of Kappa Kappa Psi (1939-1964)
- Became first full-time National Executive Secretary upon retirement from Oklahoma State University in 1955
- Lived to age 87
- Buried in Stillwater, Oklahoma
- The **A. Frank Martin Award** was established in January 1984 to honor his contributions to the band field

**Legacy:** Essential architect of the fraternity's structure and traditions through his authorship of the Ritual.

## Notable Facts & Achievements

### Educational Background
- **Diverse Majors:** The founding fathers came from various academic disciplines - Commerce & Marketing, Education, Engineering, and Agriculture - reflecting the breadth of Oklahoma A&M College
- **Graduation Years:** Class years ranged from 1920 to 1922, with most completing their studies shortly after the fraternity's founding

### Musical Talents
- **Cornet Leaders:** William Scroggs, Clyde Haston, Dick Hurst, and Raymond Shannon all played cornet - the fraternity was built on strong cornet section leadership
- **Diverse Instruments:** The group included Saxophone (Martin), French Horn (Coppedge), and Tuba (Soule) - a well-rounded ensemble

### Geographic Representation
- **Oklahoma Focus:** All hometowns listed were from Oklahoma, reflecting the fraternity's birth in Oklahoma
- **Wide Distribution:** Founders came from cities across Oklahoma - Stillwater, Sallisaw, Perkins, Nowata, Tribbey, Grove, Oklahoma City - showing the band drew from across the state

### Leadership Roles in First National Officers
1. Grand President - A. Frank Martin (1919-1922)
2. First Vice President - Raymond D. Shannon
3. Second Vice President - Clyde Haston
4. Secretary - Clayton E. Soule
5. Treasurer - Carl A. Stevens
6. National Editor and Assistant Secretary - William A. Scroggs

### Local Officers (Alpha Chapter)
1. President - William Alexander Scroggs (unanimously elected)
2. Vice President - A. Frank Martin
3. Secretary - Clayton E. Soule
4. Sergeant of Arms - William H. Coppedge

### Lasting Contributions
- **The Ritual** - Authored by A. Frank Martin, forming the spiritual foundation of the fraternity
- **The Badge** - Designed by Clyde Haston and William H. Coppedge, serving as the fraternity's visual symbol
- **The Institution** - The collective vision of ten bandsmen created an organization that has existed for over 100 years

## Legacy and Honors

### Kappa Kappa Psi's Recognition

**Bohumil Makovsky Memorial Award** - Established 1979
- Honors outstanding contributions within the fraternity
- Named after the professor who served as guiding force to the founders

**William A. Scroggs Founder's Trophy**
- Celebrates the vision and leadership of the fraternity's founder
- Perpetually commemorates Scroggs' role in bringing the fraternity into existence

### Long-Term Impact

The ten founding fathers of Kappa Kappa Psi created an organization that:
- Established the first national fraternity exclusively for college bandsmen
- Developed a Ritual and Constitution that endured for over a century
- Created a badge and symbols recognized across North America
- Inspired the formation of sister organization, Tau Beta Sigma (for women)
- Connected musicians through a common bond of brotherhood and musical excellence
- Influenced music education and college band development throughout the United States

### Fraternity Expansion

After the Alpha Chapter was chartered on November 27, 1919, the fraternity quickly expanded:
- 1920-1921: Petitions begin arriving from other institutions
- January 2, 1922: First National Convention held at Oklahoma A&M College
- Rapid growth: From one chapter to multiple chapters within the first few years
- Modern era: Now includes over 100 active chapters across North America

## Conclusion

The ten charter members of Kappa Kappa Psi - William Alexander Scroggs, A. Frank Martin, Raymond D. Shannon, Clayton E. Soule, Clyde Haston, William H. Coppedge, Dick Hurst, George Asher Hendrickson, Carl A. Stevens, and Iron Hawthorne Nelson - were college bandsmen at Oklahoma A&M College who shared a vision of unity and musical excellence.

Their legacy lives on in every member of Kappa Kappa Psi who embraces the values of **Honor, Integrity, Respect, and Loyalty** that guide the fraternity to this day.
